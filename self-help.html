You are in control. Project Evan is meant to support real-world connection, not pressure. Move at your own pace and choose what feels right for you.

1. Healthy Use Habits

Use Project Evan intentionally: set goals for what you want from your social life and focus on quality plans rather than quantity. Taking breaks is healthy and encouraged.

  • Choose one or two plans per week instead of overbooking yourself.
  • Use quiet hours on your phone to reduce notification pressure.
  • Reflect weekly: which plans made you feel good, safe, and energized?

2. Building Social Confidence

If meeting new people feels hard, start small. Confidence grows from repeated, positive experiences.

  • Start with low-pressure activities such as coffee, walks, or daytime events.
  • Invite one trusted friend to join your first few plans.
  • Use clear messages: simple, direct communication reduces anxiety.

3. Boundaries and Consent

Your boundaries matter. You can pause, decline, or leave any plan for any reason.

  • Say what you are comfortable with in advance.
  • Respect others' boundaries and expect the same in return.
  • Block or report anyone who ignores consent or makes you uncomfortable.

4. Online and Offline Safety

When meeting someone new, prioritize safety:

  • Meet in public places for first-time plans.
  • Share your plan details with a trusted person.
  • Arrange your own transport and keep your phone charged.
  • Leave immediately if a situation feels wrong.

5. Reporting Problems

If you experience harassment, impersonation, threats, or unsafe behavior, report it in-app or contact our support team. We review safety reports as a priority.

For urgent threats to life or safety, contact emergency services first.

6. External Support Resources

Depending on your location, support services may include mental health hotlines, crisis lines, and community organizations. If you need immediate emotional support, please contact a local crisis support service in your country.
